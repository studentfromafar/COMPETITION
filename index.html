Culture

Historical Influences

  • Indigenous Traditions: The archipelago's original inhabitants, such as the Igorot, Lumad, and Mangyan, have preserved unique traditions, crafts, and languages.
  • Spanish Colonization (1521-1898): Spain's 377-year rule left a significant mark, particularly in religion, language, cuisine, and architecture. Christianity, particularly Roman Catholicism, is a major legacy.
  • American Colonization (1898-1946): Introduced the English language, modern education, and aspects of Western culture.
  • Asian Neighbors: Trade and migration have brought influences from China, Japan, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

Language

  • Filipino: The national language, based on Tagalog, is widely spoken alongside numerous regional languages like Cebuano, Ilocano, and Hiligaynon.
  • English: Widely used in government, business, and education.

Religion

  • Catholicism: About 80% of Filipinos are Roman Catholic, which influences many festivals, traditions, and social norms.
  • Other Christian Denominations: Including Protestant, Iglesia ni Cristo, and others.
  • Islam: Practiced by about 5% of the population, primarily in Mindanao.

Festivals and Traditions

  • Fiestas: Colorful celebrations held in honor of patron saints, combining religious rituals with street parades, music, and dance.
  • Simbang Gabi: A nine-day series of dawn masses leading up to Christmas.
  • Ati-Atihan, Sinulog, and Pahiyas: Notable festivals showcasing local culture, dance, and elaborate decorations.

Cuisine

  • Adobo: A popular dish made with meat marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices.
  • Lechon: Whole roasted pig, a centerpiece in celebrations.
  • Sinigang: A sour soup typically made with pork or seafood and tamarind.
  • Halo-Halo: A refreshing dessert of mixed sweets, fruits, shaved ice, and milk.

Arts and Literature

  • Traditional Crafts: Include weaving, pottery, and carving, often with regional variations.
  • Modern Arts: The Philippines has a vibrant contemporary art scene, including painting, literature, and performing arts.
  • Music and Dance: A mix of indigenous, Spanish, and modern influences; Tinikling (a bamboo dance) and Kundiman (traditional love songs) are examples.

Family and Social Structure

  • Close-Knit Families: Family ties are strong, with extended families often living close to each other.
  • Bayanihan: The spirit of communal unity and cooperation.

Contemporary Culture

  • Media and Entertainment: A robust film and television industry, with Filipino stars enjoying popularity both locally and internationally.
  • Sports: Basketball and boxing are extremely popular, with Manny Pacquiao being a national hero.

Top Pinoy Must Try Foods

Filipino food culture is a vibrant tapestry of flavors, history, and tradition. Each dish tells a story of the country's rich heritage and diverse influences.

  • Halo-Halo

    The beloved summer treat Halo-halo is like an unofficial symbol of summer in the Philippines. The longing for something cold and refreshing under the sweltering heat, the sweet and surprising flavor bombs that remind us of happy times is what this colorful concoction brings to the table.

  • Pansit

    Pancit is a stir-fried noodle dish that consists of meat and vegetables. For Filipinos, pancit is a simple dish that symbolizes a long and happy life because it is usually served on birthdays or special occasions.

  • Palabok

    Palabok or Pancit Palabok is a type of Filipino noodle dish that makes use of cornstarch sticks, which is also known as palabok noodles. It has an orange sauce composed of minced pork, tinapa flakes (or smoked fish flakes), and annatto water.

  • Adobo

    Adobo is a dish that is usually made with meat (chicken, pork, or beef) marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and other spices. The meat is slowly cooked until it becomes tender and flavorful. Adobo is often served with rice and is a staple dish in many Filipino households

  • Lechon

    When serving whole lechon during a feast, the belly area of the pig is usually the one that gets consumed first. Because the stuffing is placed in the stomach, the meat there is also the one with the best flavors. Not surprisingly, the ribs and the meat around it are what most lechon fans pick on.

  • Isaw

    The dish is usually dipped in a vinegar-based sauce that is made with chili peppers and onions (sawsawan). Because it is one of the cheapest Filipino street food meals, the dish is extremely popular, and there are even street stalls called isawan, devoted entirely to the preparation of this specialty.

  • Lupiang Shanghai

    Lumpia are made of thin paper-like or crepe-like pastry skin called "lumpia wrapper" enveloping savory or sweet fillings. It is often served as an appetizer or snack, and might be served deep-fried or fresh (unfried)

  • Sinigang

    Not only is it quick and easy to make, it satisfies your umami taste buds and exceeds your daily portions of protein and veggies. Sinigang is a tamarind-based soup that is known for its sour and savory taste. You can make it with either fish, pork belly, spare ribs or corned beef.

Enjoy Our Fun Activities

Experience a World of Adventure: Uncover the Thrills of Island Hopping, Dive into Crystal Clear Waters, and Indulge in Fun Activities across the Philippines.

Surfing at La Union

San Juan, La Union has rightfully earned its reputation as a surfer's paradise. With its consistent waves, laid-back vibe, and stunning beachfront setting, it attracts wave riders from all corners of the globe.

ATV ride at Mt. Mayon

Rev up an ATV and drive all the way to the wall of volcanic debris that was formed from the confluence of a mudslide and volcanic eruption in 2006 at the foothills of Mayon. Then, climb up the jagged rocks leading up to a helipad to get a closer view of the majestic volcano’s near perfect cone.

Karaoke with locals

Music brings joy to Filipinos, but being able to perform and get enjoyment for others gives a whole lot more joy that one cannot fathom. Filipinos are known for their love of celebrations, and karaoke is the best companion to these joyous occasions.

Clear Kayak at Boracay

Another must-try activity in Boracay today is the Crystal Kayak. A photo-op activity, go ahead and snap your most unique and instagrammable shots while on the crystal kayak in the calm waters of Boracay.

Hike Mount Guiting-Guiting

Mt. Guiting Guiting is one of the toughest hikes in the Philippines and is actually the training ground for Filipinos before they head to Nepal to attempt Mt. Everest! Don’t get scared off yet, although it is a big peak for the Philippines, it stands at 2048m above sea level, which isn’t a huge peak as far as mountaineering goes.

Our Unique Transportation

Jeepney

ᜁᜁᜉ᜔ᜈᜒᜌ᜔

Locally referred to as a jeepney or jeep, this interesting-looking vehicle is an affordable mode of Philippine public transport. With designated routes, which are usually painted on their sides or displayed on their windshields, jeepneys stop anywhere along the way to pick up or let off passengers.

Tricycle

ᜍᜒᜃ᜔ᜌ᜔ᜃ᜔ᜎᜒ

The tricycle is perfect for faster commute as they can pass along alternative routes and smaller roads. They're also prohibited from traversing on highways during the day. In rural areas, trikes are the main form of transport.

Pedicab

ᜁᜇᜒᜃᜊ᜔

The pedicab, commonly known in the Philippines as the padyak or trisikad (tri-see-kad), is a three-wheeled bicycle with a covered sidecar used to transport the public in rural and suburban areas where there are smaller roads.

Kalesa

ᜀᜎᜒᜐ

The kalesa, a horse-drawn carriage, is a traditional mode of transportation in the Philippines that has become a cultural heritage and tourist attraction in some parts of the country . It has been used for centuries to transport people and goods, especially in urban areas.

I'am Pinoy

Filipinos are known for their unwavering hospitality and the spirit of bayanihan, a collective effort to achieve common goals. Additionally, asserting our sovereignty, including ownership of the West Philippine Sea islands, is a source of pride for Filipinos.
